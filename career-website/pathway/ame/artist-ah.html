Job Description

Using art, artist can use art just to express anything theyre feeling. Their are many different types of tools that different artists work with like sculptors work with 3d material like clay, stone, and wood, illustrators use pencils, charcoal, and many other materials, painters paint with paint. The subject for an artist can be anything, if its outside, animals, people, and many other things. Whatever an artist can use as inspiration just to create something, thats what being an artist is mostly about, being creative just to make anything that can inspire someone into making something or using other things as inspiration just to create something that you can call yours. "

Education and Training

No formal training is really required for artist, their are all types of art programs that teach and guide others into becoming an artist, some art programs teach about the history of art, techniques, and types of art. There is also colleges and schools that offer degrees in art and programs. No training is needed to become an artist, programs give you an opportunity to make a portfolio of all your work you have done that is your best work, to give you a better chance into getting hired/getting a job.

Earnings

According to Xello, being self-employed as an artist can really depend on how much you can make, because you only make money if someone purchases your art. Artists who find salaried positions usually earn between $19,000 and $126,000 a year. The median income is $61,000 a year. Their income can depend on many things, such as the economy. Other factors include how popular their work is and the type of people who buy their art.

Core Tasks

  • Use paints, clay, wood, charcoal, glass, or other materials
  • Display artwork in galleries
  • Find buyers for completed artwork
  • Create paintings, sculptures, carvings, or other types of art

Workplace Conditions

  • Most are self-employed
  • Work indoors in studios, and sometimes outdoors on location
  • Long, irregular hours
  • May travel to exhibit art pieces
  • Often work alone, and may talk to buyers and other artists
  • Pressure to be creative can be stressful
  • Use inks, glues, paints, and other tools

Most artists are self-employed. They work when they feel inspired and have time between their other commitments. These commitments are often other part-time jobs, including teaching art. No two days in a row are ever the same for artists.

Established artists often work for a long time on a project. When they finish, they may take a break to come up with new, creative ideas for their next work of art. Struggling artists may not be able to take these kinds of breaks. One thing all artists have in common is a desire to work on their art. They work long, irregular hours to complete it.

Artists usually work in a studio in a commercial building, or in their home. Studio spaces should be large and airy to reduce the risk of exposure to various fumes.

Hiring Opportinities

Most artists are self employed.

Sample Career Path

UNKNOWN ARTIST

Example tasks:

  1. Find opportunities to show your art
  2. Set up a studio
  3. Build up a portfolio
  4. Communicate with other artists
SEMI-ESTABLISHED ARTIST

Example tasks:

  1. Possibly start a relationship with a gallery
  2. Show art in galleries and in group shows
  3. Find opportunities to show and sell your art
  4. Establish a style or creative vision
  5. Apply for grants and awards
ESTABLISHED ARTIST

Example tasks:

  1. Continue to create art in your established style
  2. Show work in local, national, and international venues
  3. Inclusion in shows with other successful artists

Future Growth and Demands

The demand for this career is stable. The career employs a moderate number of people compared with other careers. But, its growth rate is slower than the national average growth for all careers.

About 95,400 thousand openings for arts and design occupations are projected each year, on average. In addition to new jobs from growth, opportunities arise from the need to replace workers who leave their occupations permanently.
(Bureau of Labor Statistics)
