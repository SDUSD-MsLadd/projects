User Experience Designer

by Trish Ladd

Internal Navigation

Job Description | Education | Earnings | tasks | workplace | Hiring | Career Path | Future | Citations

Job Description

User Experience designers craft beautiful and functional interfaces for websites, apps and other software to make them easier for consumers to use. Their end product may be the interface, but there is a lot of thought and research to do that goes into it. Some UX designers have a narrow role, while others are more broad. This career brings together psychology and marketing, understanding of human/computer interaction and design as well as software architecture.

Education and Training

Most employers prefer you to have post-secondary education. 2 and 4-year colleges. You can earn a degree or certificate. Some employers look for designers who have a bachelor's degree. There's no specific training required for this career, but no matter your path, a strong design portfolio is a must.

Earnings

According to xello, a UX Designer can get anywhere from $50,000 to $125,000 per year. This would depend on the location and experience of the designer.

Core Tasks

  • Gather or create the elements such as photographs, text and illustrations
  • Consult with clients or internal teams about projects and user needs Research
  • plan and create a product wireframe
  • Create prototypes and present them to teams of clients for feedback and approval
  • Use the collected elements to create a final product, like an app of web page

Workplace Conditions

  • Work Indoors in offices or from home
  • 40 hours a week, with some overtime to meet deadlines
  • Pressure to meet deadline can be stressful
  • Work for design agencies or companies with digital products, or work freelance
  • Often work alone but may talk to team members or clients

Hiring Opportinities

Glassdoor provided several companies that offer remote opportunities such as: DRT Strategies Inc., Audience, and Avex Designs LLC.

Local agencies and companies I personally know of that hire for these positions are: Intuit, Mindgroove, Miva and Red Door

Sample Career Paths

Title: Junior UX/UI Designer

Example tasks:

  1. Research and design simple prototypes
  2. Work with cross-functional teams to create products
  3. Assist Sr. level UX designers
Title: UX/UI Designer

Example tasks:

  1. Plan and run user testing sessions to get feedback
  2. Research and design complex prototypes
  3. Seek and implement team feedback
Title: Senior UX Designer

Example tasks:

  1. Manage and Mentor Designers
  2. Develop UX design standards
  3. Work with product managers and developers to research and build products based on market needs

Future Growth and Demands

Overall employment of web developers and digital designers is projected to grow 23 percent from 2021 to 2031, much faster than the average for all occupations.

About 21,800 openings for web developers and digital designers are projected each year, on average, over the decade. Many of those openings are expected to result from the need to replace workers who transfer to different occupations or exit the labor force, such as to retire. (Bureau of Labor Statistics)
