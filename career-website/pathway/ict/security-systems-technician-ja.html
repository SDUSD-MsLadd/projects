Security systems technician

by Jazmin

Internal Navigation

Job Description | Education | Earnings | tasks | workplace | Hiring | Career Path | Future | Citations

Job Description

Security systems technicians install and repair electronic security equipment. This includes fire and burglar alarms, intercom systems, and other equipment.

You should further secure the system by protecting your boundaries, equipment, network devices, and sensitive data against any attack, as well as secure sensitive data. Qualified candidates should have a background in security or systems engineering.

Education and Training

Much of the training you need to work as a security systems technician can be obtained on the job. A high school diploma may be all you need for some jobs. However, most employers prefer that you have some relevant training. In fact, it can be difficult to move forward without it.

Earnings

According to xello, New technicians usually make from $14 to $18 an hour. Experienced techs can earn from $18 to $34 an hour. For full-time techs, that works out to a range of $30,000 to $71,000 a year. The median income is about $23 an hour, or $47,000 a year.

Core Tasks

  • Install alarm systems in homes and businesses.
  • go to evaluate sites for work.
  • use the tools to cut holes in walls, floors and ceilings and then feed the cables through the holes.
  • take care of the paperwork for the client.

Workplace Conditions

  • new buildings that are under construction or some old ones.
  • 40 hours a week, 5 days a week.
  • Those employed by larger companies often work one of three 8-hour shifts a day.
  • They work for security systems companies, these can be small independent companies or national chains.

Hiring Opportinities

universal ally, securitas, ADT, HSS, convergent, Corodatos etc.

Sample Career Paths

SECURITY SYSTEMS TRAINEE

Example tasks:

  1. Learn the company's systems
  2. Install alarm systems under supervision
SECURITY SYSTEMS TECHNICIAN

Example tasks:

  1. Train customers to use systems
  2. Contact customers having systems installed
  3. Install and test systems
INSTALLATION SUPERVISOR

Example tasks:

  1. Obtain drawings, equipment, and tools
  2. Test the systems
  3. Train customers how to use systems
  4. Configure systems on paper first, and then program into system

Future Growth and Demands

The demand for this career is increasing. It is growing moderately in relation to the national average growth for all careers. Also, the career employs a fairly large number of people compared with other careers
