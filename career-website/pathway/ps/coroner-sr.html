Coroner

by Samantha R

Internal Navigation

Job Description | Education | Earnings | tasks | workplace | Hiring | Career Path | Future | Citations

Job Description

A coroner is a person who examines dead healthy bodies. Coroners have to find out the cause of death since the person was healthy. A coroner needs evidence and does an autopsy (dissecting and testing tissue samples that are removed from the body).

Education and Training

Some experience in law enforcement or legal fields. The surest way to having this career you need to be a medical doctor.(have a bachelors)

Earnings

Can earn from 30,000 starting to 300,000 with experience.

Core Tasks

  • Interview by standers (family, witnesses)
  • Investigate the deaths
  • Review police and pathologist reports Some autopsies

Workplace Conditions

  • Work for regional governments
  • Work indoors in offices, labs, or hospitals
  • 40 hours a week, and may be on call for emergencies Travel locally to visit scenes of deaths and interview witnesses Often work alone, and may talk to police officers and witnesses Risk of exposure to contaminants or diseases from dead bodies

Hiring Opportinities

County of San Diego

Sample Career Paths

Title: Entry level

Example tasks:

  1. Receive extensive on-the-job training in pathology
  2. Work under the supervision of licensed pathologists to examine tissue, blood, and body fluid samples
  3. Diagnose diseases and present results to attending physicians
  4. Work in a hospital or another medical setting
Title: Mid Level

Example tasks:

  1. Conduct autopsies on dead bodies
  2. Run lab tests on tissue samples removed from the body
  3. Determine the medical cause of death
  4. Prepare reports for the coroner
Title: Senior Level / peak of career

Example tasks:

  1. Interview relatives or witnesses
  2. Create coroners' reports
  3. Investigate deaths
  4. Review police and pathologist reports
  5. Possibly conduct autopsies
  6. Answer the five key questions about each case
  7. Testify in court
  8. May conduct inquests

Future Growth and Demands

Employment of forensic science technicians is projected to grow 11 percent from 2021 to 2031, much faster than the average for all occupations.
