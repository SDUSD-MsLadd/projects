Plumber

by Arthur Romo

Job Description

Plumbing is fixing pipes and plumbing systems along with clearing up any sewage waste to make sure thereâ€™s clear drinking water.

Education

Most plumbers learn through a 4-5 year apprenticeship program or it can also be learned in high school.

Earnings

Apprentice: 30,000-45,000
Journeyperson: 40,000-75,000
Supervisor: 60,000-95,000

Core Tasks

  • View blueprints to plan a system.
  • Cut holes in buildings to find pipes.
  • Assemble pipe sections.
  • Fix water supplies and drainage pipes.
  • Install fixtures like toilets and faucets.

Workplace Conditions

  • Work indoors in small spaces, or outdoors in all kinds of weather
  • 40 to 45 hours a week, and may be on call for emergencies
  • Work is physically demanding, including heavy lifting and carrying
  • Travel locally to different job sites
  • Often work alone, and may talk to clients or other tradespeople
  • Risk of injury from cuts and scrapes, falls, and electric shock

Hiring Opportinities

There is plenty of places that hire such as Home Depot, Lowes, and Menards.

Sample Career Paths

Title: Apprentice
  1. Registered in an apprenticeship program
  2. Possibly have your own tools
  3. Do simple tasks such as cutting pipe and easier welding jobs-all supervised by a fully-qualified plumber
  4. Prepare the job site for qualified plumbers
  5. Learn plumbing theories and practices
Title: Journeyperson
  1. Plumber's license
  2. Completion of apprenticeship
  3. Cut pipe and weld it in place
  4. Cut pipe and weld it in place
  5. Install other pipes with sealant
  6. Work independently
  7. Train apprentices
Title: Supervisor
  1. Several years of experience
  2. Install other pipes with sealan
  3. Prepare blueprints
  4. Coordinate large job sites with other plumbers
  5. Cut pipe and weld it in place
  6. Train apprentices

Future Growth and Demands

This career is in high demand. It is growing moderately in relation to the national average growth for all careers. Also, the career employs a large number of people compared with other careers.

There is an increasing demand in plumbers especially when it comes to floods, tropical storms and hurricanes or just a simple toilet fixture.
