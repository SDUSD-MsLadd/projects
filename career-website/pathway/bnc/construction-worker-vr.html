ROAD CONSTRUCTION AND MAINTENANCE WORKER

by Vale

Job Description

--> Workers in the construction and road maintenance industries maintain and fix current roadways. This employment involves a variety of duties, the most of which call for a lot of strength and endurance. Consider the amount of work required to repair a damaged highway stretch.They maintain the safety and clarity of the roads for all users. By hand, the asphalt is spread, finished, and filled with potholes. They lubricate the road and clean out the ditches.Road workers can advance to more difficult construction duties as they gain expertise.

Education and Training

High School 2-Year College or Technical Training.

Earnings

$26,030 - $84,160 from Xello.

Core Tasks

  • Place cones and signs to re-route traffic
  • Excavate, level, and grade land using earth-moving machines like bulldozers
  • Lay new roads using heavy equipment such as asphalt pavers and steam rollers
  • Repair damaged sections by filling potholes
  • Clear roads and ditches, and maintain road shoulders, signs, and guard rails

With construction Workplace Conditions will be mostly outside.

  • Work for municipal or state governments or road construction companies
  • Work outdoors in all kinds of weather
  • 45 to 50 hours a week, with some weekends and overtime
    • Work is physically demanding May travel locally to different job sites Work closely with the road crew Risk of heat stroke, insect bites, and injury from machinery

Sample Career Paths

Title: HIGHWAY MAINTENANCE WORKER

Example tasks:

  1. Driver's license and clean driving record
  2. Able to do hard manual labor and follow instructions
  3. As many tasks as needed
  4. Clear ditches
  5. Maintain road shoulders and guard rails
  6. Direct traffic
  7. Trim trees and clear weeds and brush
  8. Fill potholes
  9. Spread and finish asphalt
  10. Apply oil to the road
Title: HEAVY EQUIPMENT OPERATOR

Example tasks:

  1. Completion of related on-the-job training
  2. Years of experience on a construction team
  3. Knowledge of safety procedures
  4. Operate heavy equipment to tear up roads, grade surfaces, and pour asphalt
  5. Excavate sites for road construction
Title: FOREPERSON

Example tasks:

  1. Supervise staff on site
  2. Coordinate job schedules and staff shifts
  3. Report to company managers
  4. Make sure that all jobs proceed safely and on schedule
