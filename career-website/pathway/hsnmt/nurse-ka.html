Job Description

Nurses play a role in keeping people healthy. They assist patients who are ill or injured and they help doctors treat them. They also provide information about nutrition and exercise. Nurses combine a passion for medicine and helping and caring for others in their jobs everyday. The most well known types of nurses are registered nurses or RNs. They need more education than practical nurses. As a result they often take on a wider range of tasks.

Education and Training

To become a registered nurse you need formal training. First you need a high school diploma. You should take senior level science, math, and English courses in high school. Then you have to apply to a nursing program. The entrance requirements for a nursing program depends on the college you go to. You can contact your school and plan to attend for more details.The higher level of your education, the more advantage you'll have. All states require nurses to be licensed. You must pass a national licensing exam after graduating from a nursing program. If you want you can continue your education. You can find masterâ€™s and PhD programs at many colleges. RNs have the option of getting certified in many specialty areas.

Core Tasks

  • Perform physical exams and collect patient information
  • Provide medical treatments, such as bandaging wounds or giving medication
  • Monitor results of treatment
  • Keep patients informed
  • Maintain records and charts, and supervise other nurses and support staffs

Workplace Conditions

  • Work for hospitals, nursing homes, walk-in clinics, prisons, schools, or other medical settings 1
  • Work indoors in hospitals and clinics 2
  • 35 to 40 hours a weeks, with some evenings and weekends
  • Work may be physically and emotionally demanding
  • Often work with clients, sometimes in close physical contact
  • Risk of contact with patients who have infectious diseases

Sample Career Paths

staff nurse:

Example tasks

  • provide general patient care
  • give treatments and medications
  • perform some administrative duties
Head Nurse:

Example tasks

  • take care of patients
  • arrange work schedules
  • Assign nursing duties
  • perform other administrative duties for a specific ward, floor, or unit
  • administer medications and provide treatmeants
Director of Hospital nursing Staff:

Example tasks

  • oversee the hospitals nursing services
  • perform many administrative duties
  • assist with patient care and treatment as necessary
  • liaise with other hospitals departments

Future Growth and Demands

This career is in high demand. Its growing needs for workers is much higher than the national average growth for all careers. Also, the career employs a large number of people compared with other careers.

Why I like this career

I chose this career because I care a lot about peoples health and always believed in helping them. Ever since I was a little girl, I loved playing doctor with my dolls pretending they were my patients. As I grew up my mom noticed that I didn't get disgusted by blood or deep cuts. She always asked me what I wanna do when Im older and I always say a nurse because I like making people feel better when their hurt or ill. She told me it's good that I wanna be a nurse because I know how to deal with severe problems and health.
