Job Description

A pharmacist is someone who prepares, distributes, preserves, and formulates medicines and gives information on how to take the medicine, any side effects, administer types of medicine, and if it can interfere with any medication that the patient is already taking. The most common pharmacist jobs are community pharmacists, and hospital pharmacists. There are also research, academia, military, and government pharmacists.

Historically, pharmacists would only check and distribute drugs to doctors for their patients. Now pharmacists advise patients and physicians on dosages, interactions, and side effects. Pharmacists help monitor the health of patients to make sure they are using it safely and it is effective.

Education and Training

Education to become a pharmacist varies by country, but in the U.S, all pharmacists must have a Doctor of Pharmacy degree, and pass the North American Pharmacist Licensure Examination (NAPLEX) to enter pharmacy practice. They also must have done an internship under a licensed pharmacist (1,500-2,080 hours), passing a Multi-state Pharmacy Jurisprudence Exam (MPJE). Arkansas, California, and Virginia have their own exams instead of the MPJE.

To get into a doctorate of pharmacy program you must complete four years of pharmacy school, which include at least one year of practical experience. To graduate, most schools require students to pass a Pharmacy College Admissions Test (PCAT) and have 90 credit hours of university counsel work in sciences, mathematics, composition, and humanities before entering the PharmD program. Post-graduates might also complete a residency that is usually 1-2 years. PharmD graduates may also do a fellowship geared towards research. Fellowships are usually 1-3 years and require at least 1 year of residency.

Since 2016, all states and the District of Columbia allow pharmacists to give vaccinations.
In California, all lincensed pharmacists can perform the following:

  • Order and interperet drug therapy related tests
  • Administer vaccines in accordance to the latest CDC standards for anyone of 3+ years
  • Provide oral self-administered contraception
  • Provide travel medicatinos reccomended by the CDC
  • Provide smoking cessation aids

California pharmacists can apply for Advanced Practice Pharmacist (APh) licenses from the California State Board of Pharmacy. To qualify for an APh license, the applicant must have an activce pharmacist license, and be on the State Board of Pharmacy's good side. The license must be renewed every 2 years, and the person applying for a renewal must complete 10 hours of continuing education relating to their clinical practice. Any APh can:

  • Refer patients to other healcare providers
  • Perform patient assessments
  • Participate in the evaluation and management of a patients health condition with other healthcare prodiders

Earnings

According to the U.S Bureau of Labor Statistics, the average pharmacist yearly wage is $125,690. The average hourly wage being $60.43.

Of course the pay depends on which industry a pharmacists is working in. The annual pay for the most common pharmacy jobs are:

  • Health and Personal Care Stores - $118,570
  • General Medical and Surgical Hospitals - $132,970
  • Food and Beverage Stores - $120,150
  • General Merchandise Stores - $131,830
  • Merchant Wholesalers,Nondurable Goods - $129,180

Core Tasks

  • Providing pharmaceutical information
  • Supervising pharmacy technicians and other staff
  • Specialized monitoring of disease states
  • Counseling about over-the-counter medicines
  • Refferals to other doctors
  • Working with federal, state, or local regulatory agencies

Workplace Conditions

As a pharmacist, you might work for retail stores, wholesale, pharmacies, hospitals, or drug manufacturing companies. Pharmacists usually work indoors in pharmacies or hospitals. During their shift, they'll be on their feet most of the time.
Pharmacists talk to patients, pharmacy technicians, and doctors. Since pharmacists handle drugs and medications daily, they wear gloves, masks, and other protective gear. Pharmacists might also work night shifts, weekends, and holidays.

Hiring Opportunities

Some companies hiring pharmacists in San Diego are: CVS Health, Kaiser Permanente, Sharp HealthCare, UC San Diego, and Scripps Health

Sample Career Paths

Title: Pharmacy Intern

Example tasks:

  1. Prepare medications under a licensed pharmacist
  2. Maintain records and inventory on all drugs
  3. Provide information to patients about medicines
Title: Staff Pharmacist

Example tasks:

  1. Fill prescriptions
  2. Counsel patients
  3. Consult with physicians, nurses, and other healthcare professionals
  4. Perform administrative duties
Title: Pharmacy Manager

Example tasks:

  1. Consult with doctors, and others physicians
  2. Hire, fire, and supervise staff
  3. Counsel patients
  4. Handle many administrative duties

Future Growth and Demands

Employment of Pharmacists is estimated to grow 2% from 2021-2031. 13,600 openings from Pharmacists happen each year. Demand is projected to increase in hospitals and clinics and fewer jobs are expected in drug stores and retail pharmacies.
