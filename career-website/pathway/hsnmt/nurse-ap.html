Job Description

A nurse helps patients and families with many different things. They help with medicare plans, diets, medicine, and a lot of other things. They also are a big help to doctors getting patients ready for appointments and anything else they need. Some nurses take your height,weight, and blood pressure before the doctor comes and sees you. Also nurses keep all your records and information. They can refer you to other specialists you may need. Overall nurses have an important role in the medicine field.

Education and Training

To become a registered nurse (RN), you will need formal training. First, you need to get a high school diploma.Then, you must apply to a nursing program. You can either earn a degree or diploma in nursing. There are 2 types of degree programs. You can earn the 4-year Bachelor of Science degree in nursing (BSN). The other option is the 2-year associate degree in nursing (ADN). Diploma programs take about 3 years to complete.The higher your level of education, the more advantages you will have.All states require nurses to be licensed. You must pass a national licensing exam after graduating from a nursing program.

Earnings

According to xello, Registered nurses (RNs) can earn from about $51,000 to $107,000 a year. Those with more training can earn more. Managers and administrators generally earn the highest salaries.RNs can make more money by doing overtime work. They also receive pay for being on call. Full-time RNs generally get benefits as well as a salary. These can include health insurance, paid vacations, and paid sick days. Many employers also provide child care and offer flexible work schedules. Some employers give bonuses or pay for continuing education.

Core Tasks

  • Perform physical exams and collect patient information such as bandaging wounds or giving medication
  • Monitor results of treatments
  • Maintain records and charts, and supervise other nurses and support staff

Workplace Conditions

  • Work for hospitals, nursing homes, walk-in clinics, prisons, schools, or other medical settings 1
  • Work may be physically and emotionally demanding 2
  • Risk of contact with patients who have infectious diseases

Future Growth and Demands

The Bureau of Labor Statistics: https://www.bls.gov/ooh/ Overall employment in healthcare occupations is projected to grow 13 percent from 2021 to 2031, much faster than the average for all occupations; this increase is expected to result in about 2 million new jobs over the decade. In addition to new jobs from growth, opportunities arise from the need to replace workers who leave their occupations permanently. About 1.9 million openings each year, on average, are projected to come from growth and replacement needs.
