Job Description

Mental Health is when you help someone and help fix the state of mind they are in. Also help them with what they are going through mentally.

Education and Training

In order to be a mental health nurse you need: Attend an accredited nursing school. Take the nclex-rn exam and pass it. Earn a psychiatric mental health nurse practitioner degree in order to be able to work as a mental health nurse. Complete the required number of clinical experience hours and become a board-certified psychiatric nurse practitioner

Earnings

nurses make between $59,000 and $120,000 a year. Median earnings for those who work in psychiatric hospitals are around $76,000 a year.

Senior nurses with advanced degrees can earn more. For example, nurse practitioners can earn as much as $163,000 a year. They have at least a master's degree.

Earnings vary depending on a few factors. These can include location, training, experience, and place of work. Nurses with a master's degree earn more than those with a bachelor's or associate degree.

Full-time mental health nurses often receive benefits as well as a salary. They may get dental and health coverage, a pension plan, and paid vacation days.

Some nurses belong to unions. Their wages and benefits are negotiated on their behalf by union leaders.

Core Tasks

  • Observe and assess patients' conditions
  • Provide therapeutic care and treatment
  • Keep patients safe and help them adjust to their environments
  • Educate the community about mental health issues
  • Treatment can include counseling, recreational activities, and medication

Workplace Conditions

  • Work for hospitals, clinics, prisons, schools, or government agencies
  • Work indoors in hospitals, clinics, and sometimes patients' homes
  • 40 hours a week, sometimes on evenings and weekends
  • May travel locally to meet with patients
  • Often talk to patients, students, and other health professionals
  • Dealing with mentally ill patients can be demanding

Sample Career Paths

Staff nurse

Example tasks:

  • Monitor patients on an ongoing basis
  • Counsel patients and their families
  • Administer medication
Experienced Mental Health Nurse

Example tasks:

  • As above plus some supervisory and administrative responsibilites
Clinical Nurse Specialist

Example tasks:

  • provide specialized psychiatric nursing care
  • Oversee and train other nurses and staff members

Future Growth and Demands

Overall employment of nurse anesthetists, nurse midwives, and nurse practitioners is projected to grow 40 percent from 2021 to 2031, much faster than the average for all occupations.
