AutoMotive Service Technician

by Alexandro Calderon

Internal Navigation

This career is for people who have a passion for cars and like to figure out how different things work.

Education and Training

Most employers want you to have formal training so an automotive technology program can help you prepare to work in the field like being in a 2-year college and technical schools offer these kinds of programs. They usually last 6 months to 2 years.

Earnings

Most full-time automotive service technicians earn between $29,000 and $75,000 a year. Skilled techs, shop forepersons, and managers can earn much more. They can make up to $100,000 a year, in some cases. The median income is around $47,000 a year. Most techs earn an hourly wage. Their wages range from about $14 to $36 an hour. Their pay depends on their experience and expertise. Some techs make a commission. Their shops base it on the labor cost charged to the customer. Their earnings depend on the amount of work they complete. Most employers guarantee a minimum weekly salary.

Core Tasks

TRAINEE TECHNICIAN

  • A love of cars
  • Own some tools
  • Enrolled in training classes
  • Have work approved by senior technician
  • Work under the supervision of experienced technicians
  • Watch and assist in automotive repair

    • AUTOMOTIVE SERVICE TECHNICIAN

  • Own more tools
  • Completion of training classes
  • Repair and maintain motor vehicles
  • Work on both engine and body overhaul
  • Teach trainees when necessary
  • Knowledge of a variety of makes and models

    • FOREPERSON

  • Business courses or management experience
  • Many years of experience as a technician
  • Excellent diagnostic and repair abilities
  • Assign work duties to technicians
  • Act as a liaison between staff and management
  • Supervise certain areas of a garage
  • Provide customer service

    • SHOP MANAGER

  • Customer service experience
  • Extensive experience in diagnostics and repair
  • Oversee the operation of the business as a whole

Workplace Conditions

  • Service work is sometimes dirty and uncomfortable
  • Shops can be cold in winter months and hot in the summer
  • Techs may have to put up with loud noises
  • They also have to deal with strong fumes from exhaust and other things like oil and gas
  • If they aren't careful around tools and vehicles, they can get burns and cuts.
  • They must follow safe work practices
  • The job can also be physically demanding
  • Technicians spend a lot of time on their feet
  • They must be able to get under and inside vehicles

Future Growth and Demands

Employment of automotive service technicians and mechanics is projected to show little or no change from 2021 to 2031. Despite limited employment growth, about 73,300 openings for automotive service technicians and mechanics are projected each year, on average, over the decade. Most of those openings are expected to result from the need to replace workers who transfer to different occupations or exit the labor force, such as to retire.
