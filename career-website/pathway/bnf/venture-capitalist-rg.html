Venture Capitalist

by Richie Guerra

Internal Navigation

Job Description | Education | Earnings | tasks | workplace | Hiring | Career Path | Future | Citations

Job Description

Venture capitalists invest in businesses that have a chance to grow fast. They`ll put money into companies so they can get more gains after it. They are known for their knowledge of technology, biotechnology, and life science. They would usually work for companies that have access to a pool of money, which the name capital comes from.

This is a paragraph of information. Put as many as you need in this section.

Education and Training

You need to have good analytical and communication skills, and a bachelor's degree in economics, finance, commerce, or a related field. This also includes some Advance accounting qualifications, it can get you a lift in this. But you do need to have experience in finance and business management.

Earnings

The national salary is 80,000 on entry and 500 thousand if you`re experienced. If we look at this in a simple career path view, then you`ll be getting around 90k as an entry level, 135k as an associate, 225k as a principle and around 400k as a partner. According to the career paths that Xello offers.

Core Tasks

  • Reponsibility, you need to have this skill in order to manage your job well.
  • Good decicion making, venture capitalist often includes which companies you think are perfect for high growth.
  • Management of stress, working with tons of money can be stressful of times. Especially if you`re making deals for several hours. Burnouts can happen.

Workplace Conditions

  • Indoors, usually you would be doing this job indoors in a building.
  • Chances of you pairing up with someone is high. Teamwork would help with this job as working alone might be too difficult.
  • Traveling is also a possibility to meet up with different places and find more companies, more advantages.

Hiring Opportinities

There`s many variations of this job, however, they are hiring people at the moment such as VC Associates. Internal Applications are present as well.

Sample Career Paths

Entry level - Venture Capitalist

Tasks:

  1. Learn on-the-job under an associate
  2. Research companies
  3. Do financial analysis
Associate

Tasks:

  1. Evaluate existing financial opportunities
  2. Give advice to portfolio companies
  3. Analyze new investment opportunities
Principal

Tasks:

  1. Negotiate and manage deals
  2. Negotiate terms
  3. Manage staff, agents, and lawyers
  4. Source new investment opportunities
Partner

Tasks

  1. Manage staff, agents, and lawyers
  2. Negotiate terms
  3. Negotiate and manage deals
  4. Fundraise
  5. Source new investment opportunities

Future Growth and Demands

Growth in this career is growing at a nice rate in terms of marketing investments. There is no drastic change to the demand of this job.
