Job Description

Managers receive distress calls and dispatch emergency teams to those in need. Some vehicles are taxis, tow trucks and lorries. A dispatcher is a professional who ensures that everything runs smoothly by coordinating with customers, providing precise logistics for drivers to follow along on their routes, and coordinating delivery times. Dispatchers are the point of contact for drivers and have all the information needed to make deliveries.

Education and Training

High School 2-Year College or Technical Training

Earnings

A range of expected payment is $24,560 - $65,660

Core Tasks

  • They prioritize and relay calls to police, fire, and ambulance services
  • Advise drivers of traffic problems
  • Maintain records of calls and response

    • Sample Career Paths

    TRAINEE DISPATCHER (CALL TAKER): Entry level

    Example tasks:

    1. Pass some audio-memory retention tests
    2. High school diploma
    3. Phone skills
    4. Provide customer service
    5. Take orders (taxi) or emergency calls (911)
    DISPATCHER: Mid Level

    Example tasks:

    1. Complete call taker training
    2. Pass a written test, if required by employer 2
    3. Dispatch vehicles such as taxis, police cars, fire engines, and ambulances to wherever they are needed
    4. Advise the drivers on possible routes
    5. Keep track of where all vehicles are at all times
    SUPERVISOR: Senior Level / peak of career

    Example tasks:

    1. Years of experience as a dispatcher 1
    2. Oversee all call takers and dispatchers 2
    3. As many tasks as needed

    Future Growth and Demands

    Employment of public safety telecommunicators is projected to grow 4 percent from 2021 to 2031, about as fast as the average for all occupations. About 10,900 openings for public safety telecommunicators are projected each year, on average, over the decade. Many of those openings are expected to result from the need to replace workers who transfer to different occupations or exit the labor force, such as to retire.
