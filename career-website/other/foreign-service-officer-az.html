Foreign Service Officer

by Adrian Zamora

Job Description

A Foreign Service Officer (FSO) is a person who works for the Foreign Service, part of the US Department of State. A FSO’s job is to represent the American people and advise the government on foreign policy. FSOs have a wide range of workplaces and tasks. They can work at US embassies, consulates, and government offices. They help US citizens who have lost documents, they work with foreign officials and they have five career tracks. These career tracks are management affairs, consular affairs, economic affairs, political affairs, and public diplomacy. All career tracks handle a different set of tasks while all trying to promote American interests.

Education and Training

There are no educational requirements for this career but they strongly recommend that you get a bachelor’s degree in a field that relates to the career track you are most interested in. You are to take written, physical, and oral exams and tests to get this job. You also have to agree with the conditions of being an FSO such as traveling anywhere in the world.

Earnings

Range of pay is between $83,507 and $122,300. (from salary.com)
Range of pay for entry level is between $46,000 and $54,000. (from xello)

Core Tasks

  • Develop and implement foreign policy programs
  • Negotiate with foreign governments
  • Analyze international political and economic issues
  • Oversee the financial and operational activities of embassies
  • Replace lost ID or arrange for medical or legal help for Americans abroad (Depending on which career path you choose, these tasks can differ.)

Workplace Conditions

  • Work for the US Department of State
  • Work indoors in offices, or meet with people in the community
  • May be stationed in embassies around the world
  • Often talk to international ambassadors and officials
  • Adjusting to different cultures can be difficult

Sample Career Paths

CONSULAR OFFICER: Entry level

Example tasks:

  1. Supervise locally-hired employees
  2. Manage the processing of applications from local residents for temporary visas to the US
  3. Approve or deny immigrant visas to the US
  4. Help American citizens abroad who need emergency assistance
  5. Expertise in local laws, economic conditions, and political situations
CONSULAR OFFICER: Mid Level

Example tasks:

  1. Develop managerial experience by overseeing a consular unit
  2. Make decisions on visas and services for American citizens
  3. Resolve human resources issues
CONSULAR OFFICER or AMBASSADOR: Senior Level

Example tasks:

  1. Manage a large consular section
  2. Supervise American officers and foreign service national employees
  3. Manage a multimillion-dollar budget
  4. Speak to the press and perform other outreach tasks
