US Air Force F35 Fighter Pilot

by Sean Mendoza

Internal Navigation

Job Description

Fly The F35, accomplish combat missions in air to air, air to ground combat, combat training and other missions.

The F35 is a 5th generation multi-role fighter jet capable of vertical takeoff and landing. Created by Lockheed Martin, the F35 is the most advanced fighter jet in the world.

Education and Training

To start flight training you will need to become an officer in the Air Force. To become an Air Force officer you will need to earn a bachelor's degree in any major.

Then, you need to pass initial flight training, pass undergraduate pilot training and get assigned to a fighter, and complete specialized training.

Earnings

A US Air Force Pilot can make from $89,000 to up to $135,000.

Core Tasks

  • Combat Missions/ Training
  • Stealth Missions/ Training

Workplace Conditions

  • Deployment to different Air Force bases
  • Difficult/ Stressful Flights
  • High G-forces
  • Mission Breifings
  • Meetings

Hiring Opportinities

There are three branches of the US Military to become a F35 pilot. The US Air Force, US Navy, and the US Marine Corps
