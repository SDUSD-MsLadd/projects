Job Description

A professional soccer player is an athlete who plays soccer for a living. They undergo physical training, practice with their team and compete professionally during certain months out of the year. Typically, these athletes play for a club or a team to earn the classification of a professional soccer player

Education and Training

15-18 years , Get connected to the college coaches and join the trials and tryouts at college to get recognized. 18-21 years , Play at Division 1 universally level with coaches who can help you get into MLS. 21-22 years ,Attend the MLS SuperDraft and get selected to play professional soccer.

Earnings

The average for a professional player is $60,000. With many professional players, the salary is from $200,000 to $1,000,000.

Core Tasks

  • Physical Training
  • Maintain physical fitness through weightlifting, running, and other activities
  • Practice their sport in drills, scrimmages, and exhibition games

Workplace Conditions

  • Professional soccer players spend most of their time out on a soccer field, whether for practice or for a soccer match
  • Work indoors or outdoors, depending on the sport
  • 20 hours of training a week, with more during season
  • Risk of injury, and working in a competitive industry can be demanding
